Kurt Cobain was an iconic musician best known as the lead singer, guitarist, and primary songwriter of the band Nirvana, which played a key role in the grunge movement of the 1990s. Born on February 20, 1967, in Aberdeen, Washington, Cobain grew up in a working-class family in a small, rainy town near Seattle.
